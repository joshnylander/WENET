Proposal for Presentation
1st Annual Global Justice XML Data Model (Global JXDM) Users' Conference

Data Integration and The Criminal Justice System in Whatcom County,
The WENET Project
Whatcom County, Washington
by Robert Joshua Nylander

In March of 2001 the Global JXDM project began in an attempt to reconcile different national law and justice XML models. In November of 2002 the Whatcom Exchange NETwork (WENET) project was formally announced to the Whatcom County (Washington) Law and Justice Council. In January of 2003 the drafts of the JXDM arrived on the Internet. WENET had its solution. In April of 2003 the JXDM is officially released. In December of 2003 the WENET project, after applying for and receiving Federal grant funding, hosted its kickoff meeting. Now, two and a half years after Whatcom County first announced WENET, the project is nearing fruition.

Whatcom County would like to share our experience in developing such a project. Specifically we can tell the story of the project and the lessons learned to date. Some key areas to be addressed in a presentation are:

  1. Community cooperation – A strong “worker bee” committee
  2. Sure, we can build that
  3. GJXDM saves the day
  4. User Requirements
    1. keep our existing records management systems
    2. own our data
    3. control the security of our data
    4. reduce data duplication effort
    5. field and context sensitive security
    6. untilize established technologies
    7. survive political instability
  5. Technology
    1. Web Services
    2. GJXDM
    3. WENET Protocol (http://www.whatcomcounty.us/apps/wenet/currentprotocol.jsp)
      1. peer to peer query and XPath
      2. retrieval of GJDXM documents
      3. peer to peer discovery
      4. encryption
      5. authentication
      6. authorization
      7. auditing
  6. Vendors
  7. Where we are today
  8. Where we have to go
  9. Lessons learned
  10. Questions and Discussion
