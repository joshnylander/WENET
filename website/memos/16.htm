Abstract

This document specifies the policies and procedures used to manage, create, distribute, and revoke digital certificates used to secure the Whatcom County WENET network, specifically the http://wenet.co.whatcom.wa.us/ cluster. This document focuses on the certificates needed to implement section 7 of the WENET Protocol.

1. Certificate Authority

The Management Information Systems Committee (MIS) under the authority of the Whatcom County Law and Justice Council herby establishes a three person Certificate Authority Subcommittee (CA). This subcommittee is soley responsible for managemet of digital certificates issued by the MIS committee.

This subcommittee is granted authority to establish a set of root certificates for the Whatcom County WENET Cluster (http://wenet.co.whatcom.wa.us/), sign certificate requests for establishment of a service point within the cluster, revoke service point certificates and establish any necessary user certificate roots.

The MIS committee hereby established the Division of Information Technology (DoIT), Department of Administratives Services, Whatcom County, WA, USA as the organization unit of record for the Certificate Authority. One member of the CA shall be an employee of this organization.

2. Types of Certificates

The Certificate Authority Subcommittee establishes and recognizes five types of certificates.

2.1 CA Root

The Certificate Authority Root is a digital certificate and public/private key combo used to sign all other certificates issued by the CA. This shall be a self signed certificate. The RSA key length shall be 4096 bits. It shall be valid for 10 years from the time of creation. Its common name shall consist of "WENET Root" and the version number. Version numbers shall be used to increment future root certificates. The starting version number shall be one. The organization of record shall be DoIT. The certificate shall be used for Certificate Sign and CRL Sign. The pathlength for the certificate shall be one. CRL distribution points shall be those defined in section 3.6.
